### What do I mean

Essentially, if you happen to believe in an afterlife, you must
therefore, to some extent, have less reason to live your life, or
current life as it would be. As, once this life is over, you'll end up,
in the after life or whatever word you use for the afterlife. Hence,
there's less incentive to care about your current life, it's not just
over afterwards, it's, only the beginning to the end as they say.

Whereas, an atheist, or non-believer of the afterlife has this life and
this life only, once it's over it's over, there's no redo, repeat,
shuffle, or alike, it's just it. Whether you then think that it's just
an empty void afterwards, or that your conscious just ceases to exist,
regardless, there's nothing more to existence.

So, for an atheist (or whatever), your current life is your only chance
at anything in existence, so you might as well make the best of it, or
at least see it to the end. But at the same time, some atheists have a
very, meaningless look on existence, a look that's something like,

> "nothing matters, nothing will ever matter, if I ceased to exist then
> that's just all, there's nothing more to it"

In the sense that, while they're still willing to live their life, they
can "realize" that nothing they will ever do will matter in any way
shape or form, the death of the universe is inevitable and everything in
it will cease to exist. No matter if they wanna do anything about it or
not. Hence why even try.

That thought pattern alone would probably spiral you into roller coaster
of, *"why don't I just end it"*, but you don't, because, there's nothing
afterwards, so why end it, when you could at least just see it to the
end first, noting matter right? So why end so soon if it makes no
difference regardless.

There's probably some paradox to be found here, I don't doubt that. I
don't doubt a lot or at least some, atheists if they got the chance to
just, cease to exist without any after effect they'd likely do it.
However that would be different from suicide, in the sense that, one has
no after effect, it has no consequence and it's easy and painless.
Contrary to planning out a suicide, making yourself actually do it, and
go through with it, furthermore people would likely want to stop you.

But why would one want to live through a painful, taxing and exhausting
life when in the end you'll end up the same, only one way is painless.

I'm sure many atheists commit suicide, I'm very sure of it, but I'm also
sure many don't for the simple reason that it would never matter. At the
end of the day why do you do anything that makes you happy, either way
you're probably gonna end up in your bed, sleeping, waking up the next
day, so why even do anything that makes you happy, when it doesn't
realistically actually matter, the answer to that is probably somewhere
along the lines of the same reasons to live your life, because it
wouldn't matter either way, there's definitely people who just don't do
something that makes them happy because, *why would they*.

It all lies in, what do you care about, what do you want, need, and what
do you want to do? Do you want to give your brain the chemicals that
makes it feel happy? Or do you want it to simply, exist, until one day
it doesn't? That's really up to you in the end.

Because in the end nothing really does matter, so do whatever you want
with whatever you got.
